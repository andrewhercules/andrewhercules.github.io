About

Hello!

My name is Andrew Hercules and I am a multidisciplinary product and service designer with extensive industry experience across a range of sectors. And yes, in case you are wondering, my last name really is Hercules!

Currently, I am a Lead User Experience Designer at the Money and Pensions Service (MaPS). As a key member of the multidisciplinary and cross-functional team assigned to support MoneyHelper, I offer full, end-to-end product and service design expertise. This includes facilitating discovery workshops, conducting qualitative and quantitative user research and competitor analyses, generating research insights and artefacts, designing low- and high-fidelity solutions that respect technical and data constraints, and supervising usability testing sessions with external commercial partners.

Prior to joining the MaPS design team, I was the Product Design & Strategy Lead for Open Targets and supported the development of open-source digital tools and services that empower researchers to identify and prioritise potential drug targets. I have also worked product and service design and digital strategy roles for various organisations in both Canada and the United Kingdom.

My experience working across multiple industries has given me unique insights and abilities to create bespoke user-centred, collaborative, and insights-driven approaches to tackle complex product and service design challenges. I have delivered lectures and interactive workshops for design conferences in the UK, Switzerland, Spain, and Brazil. Additionally, I have also been endorsed by Tech Nation as a design expert as part of a successful application for a Global Talent Visa.
